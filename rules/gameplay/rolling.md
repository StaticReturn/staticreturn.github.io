# Rolling
Only roll when attempting something that is risky, difficult, uncertain, or rushed.  If a character is attempting something that is easy or moderatly difficulty and has little pressure, the character should just succeed.


### Challenge Rolls
Challenge Rolls are a method to determine if a character can perform a non-combat action.  Roll 2d6, add your skill modifier (+1 maximum), add your aid modifier (+1 maximum), and compare it to the target number (TN). If you roll below your TN you fail, if you roll exactly the TN you succeed but with a consequence, if you roll above the TN you succeed and if you roll double sixes you get a critical success.


| Difficulty | TN            |
| ---------- | ------------- |
| Easy       | 5             |
| Moderate   | 7             |
| Difficult  | 9             |
| Heroic     | 11            |
| Legendary  | 13            |


### Rolling again, after a failed challenge
All physical challenges may be repeated as long as the character’s tools and stamina are up to the task.

If a character fails a mental challenge he may not attempt the same challenge again until he gains inspiration, a insight, or a sufficient amount of time passes that the character may be considered mentally refreshed.

Social challenges may be repeated at the discretion of the dungeon master, who will judge the situation on a case-by-case basis.

### Social Challenges
Social challenges are not rolled, but described and narated by the player and dungeon master.  It should be an interaction.

#### **Social Challenge System: Easy**
First, a player should tell the dungeon master what he is trying to accomplish. Then the dungeon master should consider the disposition of the NPC(s) and tell the player the difficulty level of the goal.  Then the character should interact with the NPC(s), role-play, until the scenario is finish and the DM tells the player the outcome of his efforts.

#### **Social Challenge System: Normal**
The charcter should interact with the NPC(s) and the DM will judge the character's effectiveness.  In these cases the DM will not refer to rules about how hard the task is or how close the character is to obtaining his goal.

### Advantage
Roll 3d6 and keep the two highest dice.

### Disadvantage
Roll 3d6 and keet the two lowest dice.
